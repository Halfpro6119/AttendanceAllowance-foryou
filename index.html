What is Attendance Allowance?

Attendance Allowance is a tax-free benefit for people over 66 who need extra support due to a health condition.

This could include conditions like arthritis, diabetes, or other issues that affect daily life. Many people qualify without realising it.

It’s not means-tested, so your income and savings won’t affect your eligibility. You can use it for care, transport, heating, or anything that helps you stay independent.

You may qualify if:

  • You’re over State Pension age (66+)
  • You need help due to a health condition
  • You’ve had difficulties for 6+ months
  • You’re not already claiming PIP or DLA

You don’t need to currently receive care to qualify.

2025 rates

  • Higher: £478.40/month

Attendance Allowance is a tax-free benefit for people who have reached State Pension age and need help because of illness or disability. It is paid by the Department for Work and Pensions (DWP) in England, Wales, and Northern Ireland. In Scotland, a similar benefit is Pension Age Disability Payment (PADP).
